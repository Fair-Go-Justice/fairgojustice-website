[INTRO — acoustic guitar, solo]

[VERSE 1]
I drove forty minutes to file my form today
The registry said the form was wrong — try again a different way
The form on the website was version one-point-three
The form they needed was version two — why wasn't that clear to me?

I printed it again, drove back the following week
The clerk said "We can't accept this, it's not how we speak"
Not the language she used, but the meaning was clear:
You don't know the rules of this place, you don't belong here

[CHORUS]
The registry wall — you can't get through
The registry wall — built to confuse
Procedures and forms and filing fees stacked tall
For the people who most need justice — that's the registry wall

[VERSE 2]
The barrister called ahead, got the right form name
She's been here a hundred times — every week the same
She knows the clerk, she knows the process, she knows the floor
I've been here twice and both times I couldn't get through the door

Four-hundred-and-fifty dollars to file my application
Then I missed the deadline by one day — administrative nation
The judge dismissed it — no discretion applied
Four hundred and fifty dollars, justice denied

[CHORUS]
The registry wall — you can't get through
The registry wall — built to confuse
Procedures and forms and filing fees stacked tall
For the people who most need justice — that's the registry wall

[BRIDGE]
If legal aid existed with a navigator role
Someone to walk you through the form, not just take your toll
If court registries were funded to help self-represented
Instead of just processing paperwork — the barrier's dented

Pillar Two isn't just about lawyers in a room
It's about removing the procedural doom
That meets ordinary people at the counter, at the door
Before they've even had a chance to say what they came for

[VERSE 3]
A single mum trying to protect her kids in court
Turned away three times for a form one line short
An elderly man who can't read the legalese
Told to "seek legal advice" — with what money, please?

The registry wall is the first wall you hit
Before the bias, before the bench, before any of it
Break down that wall with real Pillar Two reform
And let justice be accessible — not something to perform

[FINAL CHORUS]
No more registry wall — let the people through
No more registry wall — built to confuse
Clear the forms, waive the fees, fund the guide
Pillar Two reform — justice shouldn't be denied!

[OUTRO — single guitar, barely above a whisper]
They told you the door was open.
They just never told you about the wall.

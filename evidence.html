Level 04 — Timeline Tunnel

Walk Through Time

A dry chronology becomes a cinematic tunnel. Each portal shows what happened, why it matters, and what the evidence record shows. The further you walk, the clearer the pattern becomes.

Stage 01

Incident — Source Event

Where the evidence trail begins. The original event that sets all custody, documentation, and disclosure obligations in motion. Date, time, location, and source are the foundation.

Stage 02

Collection and Preservation

Who collected the evidence, how it was sealed, and what integrity controls were applied. Any failure here cascades through every subsequent stage.

Stage 03

Storage and Access Control

The item enters a controlled storage environment. Every access must be logged. Every person who handles the evidence creates a link in the chain — or breaks one.

Stage 04

Transfer and Handover

High-risk stage. Sender, receiver, time, condition, seal integrity, and purpose must all be documented. A gap here is a gift to the opposing case.

Stage 05

Forensic Examination

Analysis must preserve the original, use repeatable methods, log every tool, and distinguish observation from opinion. Examiner credibility is a custody issue.

Stage 06

Disclosure and Court Use

Disclosure failures — withheld footage, missing metadata, unexplained redactions — can constitute a denial of natural justice. This is where broken chains do the most damage.

Stage 07

Reform Lesson

Every evidence failure is a reform opportunity. Mandatory audit trails, independent oversight, and transparent disclosure standards protect everyone in the system — including those accused.

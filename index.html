The Framework

Three Pillars of Justice Reform

Every broken system fails in one of three ways. Here's how we fix all of them.

01
🧾

Evidence Integrity

Chain-of-custody failures, missing bodycam footage, altered documents — when evidence disappears, so does justice. We demand mandatory independent oversight of all evidence handling from collection to courtroom.

Explore Pillar One →
02
⚖️

Judicial Accountability

An unaccountable judiciary is a corrupt one. When judges operate without independent oversight, bias and conflicts of interest go unchecked. We demand a fully independent Judicial Integrity Commission with real teeth.

Explore Pillar Two →
03
🛡️

Legal Aid Reform

Justice should not depend on the size of your bank account. With legal aid chronically underfunded, ordinary Australians face corporations and state prosecutors with no representation. That is not a fair go.

Explore Pillar Three →

The Evidence

Smoking Guns

The patterns are not random. The failures are not accidental. Read the evidence.

📹

Missing Bodycam Footage

Multiple cases across NSW and QLD where police bodycam footage was logged as recorded — but declared missing or corrupted before trial. The pattern is impossible to ignore.

Read the cases →
🧾

Chain of Custody Failures

Physical evidence logged, signed for, and later found to have no documented chain of custody. Evidence bags unsealed. Exhibits re-bagged without explanation. Judges told: "administrative error."

Read the cases →
⚖️

Judicial Conflicts of Interest

Judges ruling in cases involving former firms, personal associates, or business interests — without declaring the relationship. Complaints referred to panels of the judge's own colleagues.

Read the cases →
📄

Document Discrepancies

Witness statements returned with alterations not made by the witness. Court transcripts that differ from contemporaneous notes. FOI documents returned heavily redacted — or not at all.

Read the cases →
🛡️

Legal Aid Denial Patterns

Analysis of legal aid refusals reveals stark geographic and socioeconomic patterns. Rural applicants refused at 3× the rate of metropolitan applicants. Indigenous Australians disproportionately denied.

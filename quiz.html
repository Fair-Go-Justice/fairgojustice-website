Question 1 of 10 Access to Justice
Question 01 / 10 — Access to Justice
Have you or someone you know been unable to access legal help because of cost?
Legal aid is severely underfunded in Australia. Many people face courts alone against professional legal teams.
Question 02 / 10 — Judicial Accountability
Did you feel that the judge or decision-maker in your case was impartial and unbiased?
Judicial bias and conflicts of interest are among the most common complaints in the Australian system — yet there is almost no formal mechanism to challenge them.
Question 03 / 10 — Expert Witnesses
Were expert witnesses used in your case, and did you trust their independence?
In Australia, experts are often paid by one side — creating financial incentives to favour the party that hired them.
Question 04 / 10 — Delay & Cost
How long did your legal matter take from start to resolution?
Australian courts are chronically backlogged. Delay is a form of injustice — it drains resources and punishes the party with less money.
Question 05 / 10 — Transparency
Did you understand what was happening in your legal proceedings at every stage?
The legal system uses complex language, hidden processes, and procedural traps that disadvantage people without professional representation.
Question 06 / 10 — Media & Public Awareness
Do you believe the Australian media fairly covers legal system failures affecting ordinary people?
Major media outlets are often reluctant to cover systemic legal failures — particularly those involving powerful institutions, corporations, or the courts themselves.
Question 07 / 10 — Appeals & Review
If you felt a decision against you was wrong, were you able to appeal or seek review?
In Australia, the right to appeal exists on paper — but the cost, procedural barriers, and time make it effectively inaccessible for most ordinary people.
Question 08 / 10 — Emotional Impact
What impact did the legal process have on your mental health and wellbeing?
The psychological toll of prolonged legal battles is well-documented — and rarely acknowledged by the system that causes it.
Question 09 / 10 — Trust in the System
After your experience, how much trust do you have in Australia's legal system?
Trust in institutions is the foundation of a functioning democracy. When that trust is broken, the consequences reach far beyond individual cases.
Question 10 / 10 — What Needs to Change
If you could change ONE thing about Australia's legal system right now, what would it be?
This is about your priorities. There are no wrong answers here.
